# Presentation Outline: Aligning Geospatial Network Uganda with the FIG FFPLA Report

## Executive Summary of the FIG FFPLA Report (Pub No. 87)
The FIG report *"Fit-for-Purpose Land Administration: Status, Success, and Scaling"* emphasizes a necessary shift from rigid, traditional cadastral surveying to flexible, people-centered approaches. It focuses on:
1. **Three Core Frameworks**: **Spatial** (flexible mapping/imagery), **Legal** (inclusive tenure recognition), and **Institutional** (integrated, decentralized governance).
2. **Key Achievements**: Rapid scaling in low-resource contexts, improved gender equity, high cost-effectiveness, and direct support for the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
3. **Uganda Context Highlight**: The report explicitly praises Uganda's success in "Walking the Boundary"—empowering local Area Land Committees to use tablets and GNSS to map customary tenure affordably and inclusively.
4. **Tools for Scaling**: Leveraging UAVs/Drones, high-res satellite imagery, affordable GNSS (like Trimble Catalyst), and open-source digital platforms.
5. **Overcoming Resistance**: Shifting the mindset of traditional surveyors towards managing geospatial infrastructure and quality assurance rather than executing every single field measurement.

## Proposed Presentation Structure for Geospatial Network Uganda (GSPNET)

### Slide 1: Title Slide
* **Title:** Empowering Communities, Securing Rights: Geospatial Network Uganda & The Fit-for-Purpose Vision
* **Subtitle:** Aligning Local Action with the FIG FFPLA Global Strategy
* **Visual:** GSPNET logo alongside an image of participatory community mapping or a digital tablet in the field.

### Slide 2: The Land Administration Challenge
* **The Issue:** Traditional surveying systems are too slow, expensive, and often exclude the most vulnerable (women, youth, rural poor).
* **The Reality in Uganda:** 80% of land is under customary tenure, historically invisible in formal national registries.
* **The Gap:** We need a system that prioritizes *coverage and security* over millimeter accuracy.

### Slide 3: The Fit-For-Purpose (FFPLA) Solution
* **What is FFPLA?** A paradigm shift championed by the International Federation of Surveyors (FIG) and UN-Habitat.
* **The Three Pillars:**
  1. **Spatial:** General boundaries, aerial/satellite imagery, GNSS, "accuracy relative to purpose."
  2. **Legal:** Recognizing a continuum of land rights (e.g., Customary Certificates of Occupancy).
  3. **Institutional:** Decentralized, community-driven governance.

### Slide 4: GSPNET’s Alignment with FFPLA Core Principles
* **Flexible & Pragmatic:** GSPNET utilizes accessible technologies (UAVs, mobile webmaps) to adapt to local topographies and project needs.
* **Affordable & Rapid:** Drastically reducing the cost-per-parcel compared to traditional surveying.
* **Participatory & Inclusive:** We build tools that support local para-surveyors and Area Land Committees (ALCs), ensuring community agreement before digitalization.

### Slide 5: Ugandan Success – "Walking the Boundary"
* **The Context:** Translating the progressive 1995 Constitution and 1998 Land Act into reality.
* **The Method:** Community members walk their boundaries with local officials using digital tools. As the FIG report states: *"Technology records agreement; it does not replace it."*
* **The Impact:** Thousands of Customary Certificates of Occupancy issued rapidly, equitably, and with near-zero boundary overlaps.

### Slide 6: Enabling Tools & Technologies at GSPNET
* **Data Acquisition:** UAVs/Drones and high-resolution satellite basemaps.
* **Positioning:** Affordable, sub-meter GNSS receivers connected to mobile devices.
* **Digital Platforms:** Cloud-based data collection, 3D terrain viewers, and unified webmaps (the GSPNET ecosystem), seamlessly transitioning paper-based workflows to robust Spatial Data Infrastructures (SDI).
* **The Rule of Sustainability:** *"Don't start what you can't sustain."* GSPNET builds scalable, configurable software that avoids the trap of short-sighted, unmaintainable systems.

### Slide 7: Overcoming Resistance & Evolving the Profession
* **The Challenge:** Traditional resistance to "low-tech" or "less accurate" methods from entrenched professionals.
* **The Evolution:** GSPNET champions the shift of the surveyor's role from solely "measuring parcels" to "managing systems"—overseeing data quality, governing geospatial infrastructure, and leading capacity development.
* **Message to Professionals:** FFPLA is not a threat to your livelihood; it is an opportunity to serve the entire nation, not just the elite 20%.

### Slide 8: The Power of Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs)
* **Collaboration is Key:** No single entity can map a nation alone. 
* **GSPNET's Role:** Bridging the gap between government policy, international donors, local communities, and the private tech sector.
* **Sustainability:** Moving from short-term donor projects to sustainable business models integrated into national systems.

### Slide 9: Vision 2030 – A Call to Action
* **The Goal:** Universal Land Tenure Security for all Ugandans.
* **The Call:** We invite government agencies, NGOs, and traditional surveyors to collaborate with Geospatial Network Uganda to scale FFPLA methodologies. Let's institutionalize these innovations to build a resilient, equitable, and peaceful future.
